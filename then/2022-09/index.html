September 2022

General

I've realized that I will likely not be hitting my reading goal this year. I have started and stopped way too many books, and thus I fell behind. As of September 1st I was at 15/30, and there just isn't enough time to hit that. I am working to accept this as okay, and allow myself to just read what I want to read at the pace I want to go, as opposed to trying really hard to catch up with this arbitrary goal.

Spooky things

As fall approaches, I am getting more into witchy things and ghost stories. I have been listening to Otis Jiry's Scary Stories Told in the Dark, and they are great. I'm starting from the beginning and I'm only 3 episodes in, but number 2 and 3 were fantastic (1 was good too) and I'm really looking forward to the rest of these. After thinking a lot about the horror genre and what I like, I find that I prefer paranormal/supernatural horror and that's pretty much it. I also really like found footage stories and urban legends/folklore. I'm not a fan of gore, killers, etc. I just want a spooky story, specially ones that leave holes for the reader's imagination to fill in.

Witchcraft

I am currently learning about witchcraft and how that can fit into my life and how I can benefit from it.

This month I listening to Everyday Witchcraft and plan to start on Drawing Down the Moon soon.

Sugar

My wife is currently trying to break her addiction to sugar by doing a no added sugar (and limiting sugar per day) detox. I'm also doing no added sugar in things, and a couple of things have come from it:

  1. I have learned how impressively high the added sugar is in most foods
  2. I have lost a few pounds and am feeling good
  3. I've been in general more aware of what I'm consuming

This is usually the time of year that my sugar intake spikes between pumpkin spice lattes, Halloween candy, and Christmas treats. I made some vegan pumpkin spice sauce to make PSLs at home and I really like it!

All in all it's been great and I plan to continue limiting added sugar consumption moving forward.
