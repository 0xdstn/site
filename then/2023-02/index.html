February 2023

Birds

I went to the park to see what birds I could find and sketch some. I found 3 Canada Geese, a Mallard, and some American Wigeons. Two of the geese were grazing in the grass, and the other was keeping watch. As I sat sketching the two came within a foot of me. I continued what I was doing after snapping a couple photos. Shortly after the watcher came over to me and then started lowly hissing. It continued this until I got nervous enough to get up and walk away. I guess it decided I was a threat to the others.

The mallard was swimming around in a group of 7 Wigeons. They didn't seem to mind.

I heard an owl outside one night, and I think it was a Great Horned Owl after listening to local owl songs. It'll be cool if I can actually see one sometime.

On another night I managed to catch the sound of the owl on my Merlin app, and it is indeed a Great Horned Owl. It sounds pretty far away from my house though.

These are the birds I saw this month:

  • Canada Goose
  • American Wigeon
  • Mallard
  • Song Sparrow
  • Common Raven

Art

I've been struggling with trying to decide what I want to do with art lately. I've done what I usually do which is create Instagram accounts and try to do something like urban sketching. Through this process I have decided a couple things:

  1. I don't like drawing interiors or people. Those subjects never turn out how I want them to and I just don't really enjoy the process of drawing them.
  2. Instagram is great for inspiration, but it's exhausting and distracting and I don't need one for my art. The only goal for that would be to build a community, which I don't really need, or to eventually sell my art, which I also don't really have any interest in.
  3. I love drawing nature and documenting my life through objects and scenes.

I am going to go forward without an Instagram. Instead I set up an art blog for art I want to share.

I am going to stop trying to emulate others, drawing things I don't enjoy and instead focus on what I do like, which is sketch journaling and nature/green sketching.

My goal with art is to connect with nature and people around me.
